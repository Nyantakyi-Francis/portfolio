Background

Why My Path Is Different

I bring together software engineering, pedagogy, and community-facing product thinking, which gives my work a clearer sense of purpose than a typical developer profile.

I am building my career at the intersection of Computer Science and Pedagogy. My studies at BYU-Idaho strengthen my foundation in frontend engineering, web systems, and data products, while my education training at Mampong sharpens how I structure information for real learners and diverse audiences.

That combination shapes how I build. I do not just ask whether a product works technically. I ask whether users can understand it quickly, whether the flow teaches them what to do next, and whether the interface earns trust in real environments.

My NGO and community-facing work has pushed me further toward products with practical value: educational tools, analytics dashboards, and mission-driven websites that help organizations communicate clearly and serve people more effectively.

  • I build for learning, reporting, outreach, and other contexts where clarity matters as much as functionality.
  • I am especially interested in EdTech, analytics, nonprofit, and social impact engineering opportunities.
  • My advantage is pairing technical execution with an educator's instinct for accessibility, explanation, and user confidence.

Student Performance Analytics Dashboard

A React-based reporting experience designed to help educators move from dense result tables to clearer performance patterns, faster review conversations, and more confident academic decision-making.

  • Problem: Student results are often trapped in raw tables that are slow to interpret.
  • Approach: I built a visual dashboard flow with charts, grouped views, and clearer reporting structure for educators.
  • Impact: The product makes result-review discussions easier to navigate and supports quicker understanding of trends.

Key features: chart-based summaries, dashboard layout, education-focused reporting, and decision-support visual patterns.

  • Approach: I built an interactive quiz flow with browser-based access and a student-friendly interface.

04 API Integration • JavaScript

GeoSphere

A multi-API web application that turns location search into a richer discovery experience by combining contextual data, local news, and interactive trivia in one interface.

  • Problem: Useful location context is often split across separate services and disconnected workflows.
  • Approach: I orchestrated five third-party APIs inside one JavaScript app with async handling and unified UI patterns.
