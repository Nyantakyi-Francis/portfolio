01 · Foundations

Know your lines

First, learn the language. The ideas are small, but they appear everywhere.

Horizontal

Goes left to right. Its gradient is zero.

\(y=c\)

Vertical

Goes up and down. Its gradient is undefined.

\(x=c\)

Parallel

Same distance apart. They never meet.

\(m_1=m_2\)

Perpendicular

Meet to make four right angles.

\(m_1m_2=-1\)
Properties

A straight line...

  • is the shortest path between two points;
  • has no curves and no width;
  • is one-dimensional;
  • can extend forever in both directions.
Distance facts

Always remember

  • Distance is always positive.
  • Distance is zero only when the points coincide.
  • \(|PQ|=|QP|\).

02 · Coordinate geometry

Points, lengths & ratios

Coordinates let us measure a line exactly.

2.1

Distance between two points

Subtract the \(x\)-values, subtract the \(y\)-values, square, add, then take the square root.

Distance formula \[d=\sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2+(y_2-y_1)^2}\]
Example 1 Find \(|UV|\) for \(U(-7,23)\), \(V(6,18)\).

Substitute: \(|UV|=\sqrt{(6-(-7))^2+(18-23)^2}\)

Simplify: \(\sqrt{169+25}=\sqrt{194}\)

Answer: \(|UV|=13.93\) units (2 d.p.)

Example 2 Find \(|NR|\) for \(N(12,5)\), \(R(-3,-6)\).

\(|NR|=\sqrt{(-3-12)^2+(-6-5)^2}\)

\(=\sqrt{225+121}=\sqrt{346}\)

Answer: \(18.60\) units (2 d.p.)

Quick example Find the distance from \((1,2)\) to \((4,6)\).

\(d=\sqrt{(4-1)^2+(6-2)^2}=\sqrt{9+16}\)

Answer: \(5\) units

2.2

Midpoint of a line segment

A midpoint divides a segment into two equal parts. Average the two \(x\)-values and the two \(y\)-values.

Midpoint formula \[M=\left(\frac{x_1+x_2}{2},\frac{y_1+y_2}{2}\right)\]
Example 3 Find the midpoint of \(K(16,24)\), \(G(-8,10)\).

\(M=\left(\frac{16+(-8)}2,\frac{24+10}2\right)\)

\(M=\left(\frac82,\frac{34}2\right)\)

Answer: \(M(4,17)\)

Example 4 Find the midpoint of \(R(1,17)\), \(P(0,4)\).

\(M=\left(\frac{1+0}2,\frac{17+4}2\right)\)

Answer: \(M(0.5,10.5)\)

Example 5 \(W(-2,1)\) is the midpoint of \(D(-5,4)\) and \(V\). Find \(V\).

\(-2=\frac{-5+x_V}{2}\Rightarrow x_V=1\)

\(1=\frac{4+y_V}{2}\Rightarrow y_V=-2\)

Answer: \(V(1,-2)\)

2.3

Dividing a line in a ratio

Internal means the point is between the endpoints. External means it is outside them.

Internal division \(m:n\) \[P=\left(\frac{mx_2+nx_1}{m+n},\frac{my_2+ny_1}{m+n}\right)\]
External division \(m:n\) \[Q=\left(\frac{mx_2-nx_1}{m-n},\frac{my_2-ny_1}{m-n}\right)\]
External division tip: negate the smaller ratio number. Also, \(m\ne n\).
Example 6 Divide \(F(13,9)\), \(H(6,-17)\) in \(11:3\).

Internally: \(G=\left(\frac{11(6)+3(13)}{14},\frac{11(-17)+3(9)}{14}\right)\)

\(G=(7.5,-11.43)\)

Externally: \(K=\left(\frac{11(6)-3(13)}8,\frac{11(-17)-3(9)}8\right)\)

Answer: \(K=(3.375,-26.75)\)

Example 7 Divide \((-4,3)\), \((6,-12)\) in \(3:2\).

Internally: \(\left(\frac{3(6)+2(-4)}5,\frac{3(-12)+2(3)}5\right)=(2,-6)\)

Externally: \(\left(\frac{3(6)-2(-4)}1,\frac{3(-12)-2(3)}1\right)\)

Answers: \((2,-6)\) and \((26,-42)\)

03 · Equations

Describe any straight line

The gradient tells us direction. The intercept tells us position.

3.1

Gradient (slope)

Gradient is rise divided by run. Positive rises right; negative falls right.

Gradient formula \[m=\frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}=\frac{y_1-y_2}{x_1-x_2} =\frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}\]

\(m=3\): rise 3 for every 1 right.

\(m=-\frac12\): fall 1 for every 2 right.

\(m=0\): horizontal line.

3.2

Forms of a line equation

Choose the form that matches the information given.

Point-slope form\[y-y_1=m(x-x_1)\]
Slope-intercept form\[y=mx+c\]
General form\[ax+by+c=0\]
See why \(y=mx+c\)

\(\frac{y-y_1}{x-x_1}=\frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}=m\)

\(y-y_1=m(x-x_1)\)

\(y-y_1=mx-mx_1\)

\(y=mx-mx_1+y_1\)

Let \(c=-mx_1+y_1\). Therefore, \(\boxed{y=mx+c}\).

Horizontal \(m=0\), so \(y=c\).

Vertical \(m\) is undefined, so \(x=c\).

3.3

Parallel & perpendicular equations

Parallel lines copy the gradient. Perpendicular lines use its negative reciprocal.

Parallel\[m_2=m_1\]
Perpendicular\[m_2=-\frac1{m_1},\qquad m_1m_2=-1\]
Example 8a Equation through \(S(4,-2)\), \(T(8,4)\).

\(m=\frac{4-(-2)}{8-4}=\frac32\)

\(y+2=\frac32(x-4)\)

\(2y+4=3x-12\)

Answer: \(3x-2y-16=0\)

Example 8b Perpendicular through \(W(-7,5)\).

\(m=-\frac23\), so \(y-5=-\frac23(x+7)\)

Answer: \(2x+3y-1=0\)

Example 8c Parallel to 8b through \(L(2,9)\).

\(m=-\frac23\), so \(y-9=-\frac23(x-2)\)

Answer: \(2x+3y-31=0\)

3.4

Distance from a point to a line

The shortest path from a point to a line is perpendicular to the line.

For point \((x_1,y_1)\) and line \(ax+by+c=0\) \[D=\frac{|ax_1+by_1+c|}{\sqrt{a^2+b^2}}\]
Example 9 From \(J(1,5)\) to \(5x+12y+7=0\).

\(D=\frac{|5(1)+12(5)+7|}{\sqrt{5^2+12^2}}\)

\(D=\frac{72}{13}\)

Answer: \(5.54\) units (2 d.p.)

Example 10 From \(C(6,-2)\) to \(3x-4y=12\).

Write the line as \(3x-4y-12=0\).

\(D=\frac{|3(6)-4(-2)-12|}{\sqrt{3^2+(-4)^2}}=\frac{14}{5}\)

Answer: \(2.8\) units

04 · Intersections

Where lines meet

Find the meeting point, then measure the smaller angle.

4.1

Point of intersection

At the meeting point, both equations have the same \(x\) and \(y\).

For \(y=m_1x+c_1\) and \(y=m_2x+c_2\) \[m_1x+c_1=m_2x+c_2\]
Example 11 \(y=7x-13\) and \(y=-2x+6\).

\(7x-13=-2x+6\Rightarrow9x=19\Rightarrow x=\frac{19}{9}\)

\(y=7\left(\frac{19}{9}\right)-13=\frac{16}{9}\)

Intersection: \(\left(\frac{19}{9},\frac{16}{9}\right)\)

Example 12 Routes through \((2,5),(8,17)\) and \((3,20),(9,2)\).

Route A: \(m_A=2\), so \(y=2x+1\).

Route B: \(m_B=-3\), so \(y=-3x+29\).

\(2x+1=-3x+29\Rightarrow x=\frac{28}{5}\).

Intersection: \(\left(\frac{28}{5},\frac{61}{5}\right)\)

4.2

Acute angle between two lines

The acute angle is the smaller angle: greater than \(0^\circ\), less than \(90^\circ\).

Angle formula \[\tan\theta=\left|\frac{m_1-m_2}{1+m_1m_2}\right|,\qquad \theta=\tan^{-1}\left|\frac{m_1-m_2}{1+m_1m_2}\right|\]
If \(1+m_1m_2=0\), the lines are perpendicular and \(\theta=90^\circ\).
Example 13 Slopes \(4\) and \(\frac27\).

\(\tan\theta=\left|\frac{4-\frac27}{1+4(\frac27)}\right|=\frac{26}{15}\)

\(\theta=\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{26}{15}\right)\)

Answer: \(60.02^\circ\)

Example 14 \(y=19x-5\) and \(y=-6x+4\).

\(m_1=19,\;m_2=-6\)

\(\tan\theta=\left|\frac{19-(-6)}{1+19(-6)}\right|=\frac{25}{113}\)

Answer: \(\theta=12.48^\circ\)

07 · Check yourself

Review questions

Try each one before opening the answer.

01 Find the distance between \(F(3,7)\) and \(Y(-2,8)\).

\(|FY|=\sqrt{(-2-3)^2+(8-7)^2}=\boxed{\sqrt{26}}\).

02 Find the midpoint of \(R(3,5)\) and \(G(11,7)\).

\(M=\left(\frac{3+11}2,\frac{5+7}2\right)=\boxed{(7,6)}\).

03 For \(A(3,2),B(8,11),C(14,5)\), divide \(AB\) in \(3:2\), \(BC\) in \(4:1\), then find line \(PQ\).

\(P=(6,\frac{37}{5})\), \(Q=(\frac{64}{5},\frac{31}{5})\). Thus \(\boxed{15x+85y-719=0}\). It does not bisect a side because neither division ratio is \(1:1\).

04 \(M(12,-5)\) divides \(A(-6,4)\), \(B(2,0)\) externally in \(9:n\). Find \(n\).

Using either coordinate in the external formula gives \(\boxed{n=5}\).

05 Find the equation through \(D(25,6)\), \(C(29,-14)\).

\(m=-5\), so \(\boxed{y=-5x+131}\).

06 Through \(M(4,2)\), \(T(-8,-2)\): \(3y=ax+b\). Find \(a,b\).

The line is \(y=\frac13x+\frac23\), so \(3y=x+2\). \(\boxed{a=1,b=2}\).

07 Find the line perpendicular to \(5x-3y-18=0\) through \((-5,6)\).

The perpendicular gradient is \(-\frac35\). Answer: \(\boxed{3x+5y-15=0}\).

08 Find the line parallel to \(3x+5y=108\) through \((5,10)\).

Keep the same \(x,y\) coefficients: \(\boxed{3x+5y=65}\).

09 Find the acute angle between \(y=2x+10\) and \(y=-5x+4\).

\(\tan\theta=\left|\frac{2-(-5)}{1+2(-5)}\right|=\frac79\). Thus \(\boxed{\theta=37.87^\circ}\).

10 Distance from \(B(-1,-7)\) to the line through \(E(6,-4)\), \(Y(9,-5)\).

The line is \(x+3y+6=0\). \(D=\frac{16}{\sqrt{10}}=\boxed{5.06\text{ units}}\).

11 Equation through \((3,6)\), \((1,2)\) in point-slope form.

\(m=2\), so \(\boxed{y-6=2(x-3)}\).

12 Main Street is \(y=2x+3\). Find a parallel line through \((4,1)\), a perpendicular line through \((1,5)\), and the intersection of the perpendicular with a parallel through \((2,3)\).

Parallel: \(y=2x-7\). Perpendicular: \(y=-\frac12x+\frac{11}{2}\). New parallel: \(y=2x-1\). Intersection: \(\boxed{(\frac{13}{5},\frac{21}{5})}\).

13 Compare roads A: \(y=-12x+10\), B: \(-x+12y=56\), C: \(12x=76-y\), D: \(12y=x-52\).

A and C are parallel. B and D are parallel. Every A/C line is perpendicular to every B/D line.

08 · Words to know

Mini glossary

Straight line
The shortest path between two points; it has no curve.
Gradient
A measure of steepness: change in \(y\) divided by change in \(x\).
Parallel
Lines with equal gradients that never meet.
Perpendicular
Lines that meet at exactly \(90^\circ\).
Acute angle
An angle greater than \(0^\circ\) but less than \(90^\circ\).
Obtuse angle
An angle greater than \(90^\circ\) but less than \(180^\circ\).
